Career Plans

Jake's Ideal Job: Cyber Security Manager

Anthony's Ideal Job: Business systems analyst

Jessica's Ideal Job: Application support developer

Angus's Ideal Job: IT manager

Chunning's Ideal Job: Mobile application developer

Ty's Ideal Job: Chief Technology Officer

Due to the diverse nature of our group, every member had a different role that they want to specialise in. However, majority are interested in working for a large organisation using their IT expertise. This comes as no surprise due to the fact that a large firm will have more resources at your disposal, allowing you to complete your tasks as efficiently and effectively as possible. The differences in career plans lie in which division they are most interested in working in, as a result of either career interests or skill-set they currently possess. p>

Due to Anthony’s background in business, it has provided him with the skill-set to work as a business systems analyst, as he has the perspective of both a business analyst as well as a IT analyst. This role would lean more towards the creation of new systems and the implementation of them to meet the desired business goals.

Jake’s ideal job would be linked to the security division of an organisation. Due to this, he would be able to work alongside any of the other team members as his job could entitle him to provide cyber security assistance to other divisions of the firm depending on the business’ needs.

Jessica’s role as an application support developer would mean that she would be working at the front-of-house, providing support to customers who have inquiries and issues. Her division would also rotate around this and back-of-house activities such as deployment of new technologies, thus exposing her to other divisions of IT within the organisation.

Angus’ ideal job as a IT manager means that his roles would include establishing and managing the technology in a corporate business. The vagueness of this serves as an advantage as it allows him to gather feedback from other divisions of IT in order to make educated decisions based on the information provided to him.

Chunning’s ideal job is to be a mobile application developer. This means that he will work in a team of various individuals to create an app for prospective clients. He would have to go through the phases of analysis, designing, development and evaluation in order to develop a worthwhile application that is both unique and beneficial to its users.

Ty’s ideal job is to become a chief technology officer (CTO) of a large firm. This is the highest role in the IT chain, meaning that he would have to have a plethora of experience in the other division of IT before working his way up to becoming a CTO. He would largely be in control of what type of technology is being researched, and the time frame for each project.

A majority of our ideal jobs require similar skill-sets such as good communication skills, experience in the leadership roles, planning and time management skills. Most of these are gathered through experience in either work or university group work. Alongside this are various basic IT skills that are needed in our ideal roles. It can be seen that any IT job that is to be undertaken will require mainly the same skill-set, thus we can see that even though our ideal occupations are quite different from each other’s, cross-examination and feedback through group work will allow for a greater efficiency when approaching our new projects in the future.
